📖 Key Retinal Anatomy for Glaucoma Detection

Optic Disc

The bright circular region where the optic nerve exits the eye. In glaucoma, the disc margin becomes pale and cupped. AI analyzes disc size and pallor.

Optic Cup

The central depression within the optic disc. An enlarged cup (high CDR) is the hallmark of glaucomatous damage. Normal CDR < 0.5.

Neuroretinal Rim

The rim of neural tissue between disc edge and cup. ISNT rule: Inferior>Superior>Nasal>Temporal. Rim thinning indicates nerve loss.

Disc Hemorrhage

Splinter-shaped flame hemorrhages at the disc edge, strongly predictive of glaucoma progression. AI detects these subtle red flame patterns.

RNFL (Nerve Fiber Layer)

The retinal nerve fiber layer produces a bright reflex in the superior/inferior arcuate areas. RNFL defects appear as dark wedge-shaped areas.

Peripapillary Atrophy

Beta-zone PPA (zone of absent RPE/choriocapillaris) around the disc margin correlates with glaucoma severity and progressive cupping.
